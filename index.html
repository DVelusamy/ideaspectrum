Method 01

IdeaDustbin

Structured incubation for innovation thinking
MBA students move too quickly from idea to solution. IdeaDustbin introduces deliberate incubation — a structured pause where fragments of thinking are deposited, sealed, and revisited later. The composting metaphor: unfinished thoughts become the substrate for stronger ideas. No AI required.

Method 02

ICLE Framework

Idea Concept Lens Evolution — Multidisciplinary idea expansion
Most ideation methods produce breadth without depth. ICLE uses 14 conceptual lenses across 5 stages to refract a single idea through multiple domains — from numbers and structure, through living systems and human behaviour, to meaning and cosmic-scale futures. Published white paper (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19024288). Works as a physical card deck.

The ICLE Complexity Scale measures how far an idea has evolved: from Seed (ICLE-1) through Structural, Living, Meaningful, to Cosmicle (ICLE-5). This gives educators a way to assess idea maturity.

Method 03

Graffitive

Structured intellectual trespass — Write over what you know
Most innovation methods assume collaboration should be polite. Graffitive introduces productive conflict. Students write directly over each other’s ideas using five Spray Cans. The result is a layered palimpsest — a visible record of how ideas evolve through challenge. Graffitive turns critique into a creative act.

Runs as a 90-minute workshop. Students work on shared canvases with different colours for each layer. Faculty can facilitate using the methodology guide.

Method 04

Socratic AI Companion

MBA Dog Bot — AI that asks questions before giving answers
Students increasingly use AI to generate answers. MBA Dog Bot does the opposite: it operates in Socratic mode by default, prompting students to clarify assumptions, explore implications, and connect ideas. Faculty control what the AI can reference. AI as thinking partner, not shortcut.
